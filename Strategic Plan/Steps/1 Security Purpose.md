## 1. Security Purpose (Why Security Exists)

**Goal:** Explain why security matters to the business (not technology).

**How to do it**
- Read the company mission, vision, and business goals  
- Translate them into security language  

**Example**
> The purpose of the security program is to protect customer trust, ensure business continuity, meet regulatory obligations, and enable safe business growth.

**Output**
- 2â€“3 sentences  
- Non-technical  
- Business-focused  


### Examples

| Industry | Security Purpose (Why Security Exists) | Business Value |
|--------|----------------------------------------|----------------|
| Banking / Financial Services | Protect customer trust and financial assets while ensuring uninterrupted access to banking services. Support regulatory compliance and safe growth of digital financial offerings. | Preserves customer confidence, avoids regulatory penalties, and enables secure digital expansion. |
| Healthcare | Protect patient privacy and ensure reliable delivery of clinical and healthcare services. Support compliance obligations and safe use of digital health systems. | Improves patient trust, reduces legal risk, and ensures continuity of care. |
| E-Commerce / Retail | Protect customer information and maintain confidence in online transactions. Ensure continuity during high-volume sales and market expansion. | Increases customer loyalty, reduces revenue loss from outages, and supports scalable growth. |
| Technology / SaaS | Safeguard customer data and platform availability. Enable innovation through secure delivery of new features and integrations. | Strengthens customer retention, accelerates product innovation, and reduces service disruptions. |
| Manufacturing | Protect production operations and intellectual property from disruption. Support safe adoption of digital and automated manufacturing processes. | Minimizes downtime, protects competitive advantage, and improves operational efficiency. |
| Telecommunications | Ensure reliable and secure communication services and protect critical network infrastructure. Support regulatory and availability requirements. | Maintains service reliability, reduces operational risk, and protects brand reputation. |
| Energy / Utilities | Protect critical infrastructure and ensure continuous delivery of essential services. Reduce operational risk and support regulatory compliance. | Prevents service outages, protects public safety, and ensures regulatory confidence. |
| Government / Public Sector | Protect public services, citizen data, and institutional trust. Ensure service availability and operational resilience. | Builds public trust, ensures continuity of services, and reduces national and legal risk. |
| Education | Protect student and staff information while ensuring access to learning systems. Support academic operations and secure digital collaboration. | Enables uninterrupted learning, protects institutional reputation, and reduces data breach impact. |
| Logistics / Transportation | Ensure secure and reliable transportation and supply chain operations. Protect customer data and business continuity across distributed operations. | Reduces delivery disruptions, improves customer satisfaction, and supports global scalability. |
