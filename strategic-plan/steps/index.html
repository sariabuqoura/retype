# Security Strategic

# 1. Security Purpose (Why Security Exists)

Goal: Explain why security matters to the business (not technology).

How to do it

  • Read the company mission, vision, and business goals
  • Translate them into security language

Example

The purpose of the security program is to protect customer trust, ensure business continuity, meet regulatory obligations, and enable safe business growth.

Output

  • 2–3 sentences
  • Non-technical
  • Business-focused

# 2. Business Context (What You Are Protecting)

Goal: Align security with how the company actually operates.

Document

  • Business model (SaaS, retail, fintech, services, etc.)
  • Critical assets:
    • Customer data
    • Revenue-generating systems
    • Intellectual property
  • Regulatory drivers (if any): ISO, PCI, GDPR, local laws

Output

  • Short business overview
  • List of “crown jewels”

# 3. Security Scope & Functions

Goal: Clarify what security is responsible for.

Typical Security Functions (Small Company)

  • Governance & policy
  • Risk management
  • Asset management
  • Identity & access management
  • Infrastructure & application security
  • Incident response
  • Third-party risk
  • Awareness & training

Output

  • One-page scope definition
  • What security owns vs supports

# 4. High-Level Risk Assessment

Note: Mandatory for a strategic plan.

How (Lightweight)

  • Identify top risks (10–15 max)
  • Rate each risk:
    • Likelihood: Low / Medium / High
    • Impact: Low / Medium / High
  • Map risks to business impact

Example Risks

  • Data breach of customer PII
  • Cloud misconfiguration
  • Ransomware
  • Insider misuse
  • Third-party compromise
  • Regulatory non-compliance

Output

  • Simple risk register (table)
  • Highlight top 5–7 strategic risks

# 5. Security Vision (5-Year Target State)

Goal: Define where security should be in five years.

Example Vision

By year five, security will be risk-driven, embedded in business processes, aligned with industry standards, and capable of supporting growth without slowing the business.

Focus On

  • Maturity, not tools
  • Predictable and measurable security

# 6. Strategic Security Objectives (3–6 Only)

Definition: Long-term goals, not projects.

Examples

  • Establish a formal risk-based security governance model
  • Reduce high-risk findings year-over-year
  • Achieve compliance with ISO 27001 / NIST CSF
  • Integrate security into product development
  • Improve incident detection and response capability

Rule

  • Each objective must support a business goal

# 7. Strategic Initiatives (How Objectives Are Achieved)

Guideline

  • Each objective has 2–4 initiatives

Example Objective: Improve risk management
Initiatives

  • Implement annual enterprise risk assessment
  • Create a security exception and risk acceptance process
  • Report top risks to management quarterly

Output

  • Initiatives mapped to objectives
  • No tools mentioned

# 8. Multi-Year Roadmap (3–5 Years)

Goal: Turn strategy into phased execution.

Example Roadmap

  • Year 1: Foundations (policies, risk assessment, asset inventory)
  • Year 2: Control improvement (IAM, logging, backups)
  • Year 3: Monitoring & response maturity
  • Year 4: Automation & optimization
  • Year 5: Continuous improvement

Output

  • High-level timeline (not a project plan)

# 9. Metrics & Success Criteria

Principle: Measure outcomes, not activity.

Examples

  • % of critical assets with defined owners
  • Number of high risks accepted vs mitigated
  • Incident detection time (MTTD)
  • Compliance audit results
  • Security awareness completion rate

# 10. Governance & Review Model

Define

  • Strategy owner
  • Review authority
  • Review frequency

Example

  • Strategy reviewed annually
  • Risk assessment updated yearly
  • Adjusted when business changes

# 11. Final Strategic Plan Structure

Your final Security Strategic Plan should include:

  1. Executive Summary
  2. Security Purpose & Vision
  3. Business Context
  4. Security Scope & Functions
  5. Risk Assessment Summary
  6. Strategic Objectives
  7. Strategic Initiatives
  8. Multi-Year Roadmap
  9. Metrics & KPIs
  10. Governance & Review

# Key Rule for Small Companies

  • Strategy = direction
  • Policies = rules
  • Roadmaps = execution
